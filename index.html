FEATURED ARTIST

George Kusunoki Miller, professionally known as Joji and fomerly played as the characters Filthy Frank and Pink Guy, is a Japanese singer-songwriter, rapper, internet personality, and record producer. Joji's music has been described as a mix between R&B, lo-fi, and trip hop.

Miller started off his career on Youtube in 2011, as Filthy Frank, after shortly moving to United States, gaining recognition for his oddly weird characters on his comedy channels TVFilthyFrank, TooDamnFilthy, and DizastaMusic. The channels, which featured comedy hip hop, rants, extreme challenges, and ukulele and dance performances, are noted for his dark humor. Miller's videos helped popularize the Harlem Shake, which is a meme during that time. As Pink Guy, Miller released two albums, "Pink Season" and "Pink Season: The Prophecy" as well as an extended play between 2014 and 2017.

In 2017, Miller ended The Filthy Frank Show to pursue a music career under the name Joji. His debut album (on Spotify), In Tongues, was released on November 2017. His second album, Ballads 1, was released in 2018 and featured the single "Slow Dancing in the Dark". His third album, Nectar (2020), contained the singles, which everyone knows and loves, "Sanctuary" and "Gimme Love". In 2022, he released the US Billboard Hot 100 top-ten single "Glimpse of Us", his highest-charting song, which was later featured on his latest album, Smithereens (2022).
